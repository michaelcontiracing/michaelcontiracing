What is Esports?

Competitive, engaging, captivating, and explosive are all words that best describe the Esports industry. Any game or simulation that holds an organized competition that players around the world can compete in is considered an Esport. A term not new to gamers, Esports have been around for over a decade, with titles such as League of Legends, Call of Duty, and World of Warcraft pioneering the early push to make Esports a more widely understood category of gaming. Titles such as iRacing and NASCAR Heat have spearheaded the efforts to establish sim-racing as a type of Esport. This push has produced massive gains in sim-racing popularity over the last several years.

What is iRacing?

The eNASCAR iRacing World Championship Series is iRacing’s premier eSport World Championship series, officially sanctioned by NASCAR, and features the world’s most elite oval sim-racers. We compete on simulated versions of the actual real-world NASCAR tracks, with cars that are modeled to be within millimeter accuracy to their real-life counterparts.
